# Grand Slam Offer Creation Framework

Framework for creating offers so good that people feel stupid saying no. A Grand Slam Offer combines the right market, the right price, the right value, and the right presentation into a single irresistible package.

## Core Principle

The offer is the #1 lever in any business. A Grand Slam Offer delivers so much value at such an attractive price that the prospect would feel foolish declining. It maximizes Dream Outcome and Perceived Likelihood of Achievement while minimizing Time Delay and Effort & Sacrifice.

## Scoring

**Goal: 10/10.** Rate offers 0-10 based on adherence to the framework. Always provide the current score and specific improvements needed to reach 10/10.

## The Grand Slam Offer Framework

### 1. The Value Equation

Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice). Maximize the numerator, minimize the denominator.

See: [references/value-equation.md](references/value-equation.md) and [references/framework-details.md](references/framework-details.md) for levers, applications, and copy patterns.

### 2. The Grand Slam Offer

A complete package: core offer + bonuses + guarantee + scarcity + urgency + compelling name. Bundle multiple value elements so price comparison becomes impossible. You become a category of one.

See: [references/grand-slam-offers.md](references/grand-slam-offers.md) for the full assembly process and problem-solution mapping.

### 3. Finding Your Starving Crowd

Find a market with massive pain, purchasing power, easy targeting, and growth. Niching down increases perceived value because specificity signals expertise.

See: [references/starving-crowd.md](references/starving-crowd.md) for market selection criteria and the niche scorecard.

### 4. Value-Based Pricing

Charge based on value delivered, not cost. Target a 10:1 value-to-price ratio. Premium pricing signals quality, attracts better customers, and funds better delivery.

See: [references/pricing-strategy.md](references/pricing-strategy.md) for frameworks and anchoring techniques.

### 5. Bonuses: Value Stacking

Each bonus addresses a specific objection. Assign honest dollar values. Stack order matters: most valuable first. High value / low cost to deliver.

See: [references/bonuses-stacking.md](references/bonuses-stacking.md) for design frameworks and stacking strategies.

### 6. Guarantees: Reversing Risk

Five types: unconditional, conditional, anti-guarantee, implied, performance-based. Stronger guarantees reduce refund rates. Name your guarantee. Stack multiples to reverse multiple risk types.

See: [references/guarantees.md](references/guarantees.md) for the five types, naming strategies, and stacking approaches.

### 7. Scarcity and Urgency

Scarcity limits quantity; urgency limits time. Cohort-based models are the most ethical form. Fake scarcity destroys trust permanently.

See: [references/scarcity-urgency.md](references/scarcity-urgency.md) for ethical patterns and evergreen urgency techniques.

### 8. Naming the Offer (MAGIC Formula)

**M**agnetic reason why, **A**vatar, **G**oal, **I**ndicate time frame, **C**ontainer word. Not every name uses all five elements. Test names before committing.

See: [references/naming-offers.md](references/naming-offers.md) for the full MAGIC breakdown and 20+ examples.

## Offer Creation Process

1. **Identify your starving crowd.** Score markets on pain, purchasing power, targeting, and growth.
2. **Define the Dream Outcome.** State it in the customer's words.
3. **List every obstacle.** Problems, fears, objections, friction points.
4. **Create solutions for each obstacle.** Choose delivery vehicles (1-on-1, group, DIY, done-for-you, software).
5. **Trim & Stack.** Remove low-value/high-cost. Keep high-value/low-cost.
6. **Set value-based pricing.** Price at 10-20% of the Dream Outcome's value.
7. **Design bonuses.** Name each, assign defensible dollar values.
8. **Choose your guarantee.** Name it. Make it specific and bold.
9. **Add ethical scarcity and urgency.** Real limits only.
10. **Name the offer using MAGIC.** Test 3-5 variations.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Selling a commodity | Bundle unique value to become a category of one |
| Pricing based on cost | Price based on the Dream Outcome value (10:1 rule) |
| No guarantee | Add risk reversal -- stronger guarantees reduce refunds |
| Vague bonuses | Name each, describe value, assign dollar amounts |
| Fake scarcity | Only use 100% real and verifiable scarcity |
| Generic naming | Use the MAGIC formula |
| Targeting everyone | Narrow your avatar until it feels uncomfortable |

## Quick Diagnostic

| Question | If No | Action |
|----------|-------|--------|
| 10x perceived value? | Feels overpriced | Add bonuses or increase Dream Outcome |
| Starving crowd? | Hard to sell | Switch markets or narrow further |
| Guarantee reverses risk? | Prospect hesitates | Add a guarantee that makes "yes" safe |
| 3+ named bonuses? | Offer feels thin | Create objection-targeting bonuses |
| Real reason to act now? | "I'll think about it" | Add ethical scarcity/urgency |
| Competitor can't replicate? | Commodity | Bundle unique elements |
| Name communicates who + what? | Low self-selection | Rename using MAGIC |
